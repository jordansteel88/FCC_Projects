Dave Brubeck

The Ambassador of Cool

Dave Brubeck, circa 1955

GETTY IMAGES/METRONOME

  • 1920: Born in Concord, CA
  • 1944: While enlisted in the U. S. Army, Brubeck led The Wolfpack Band, the military's first integrated jazz band.
  • 1949: Formed The Dave Brubeck Octet with frequent collaborator and "Take Five" composer, Paul Desmond.
  • 1951: Organized the first iteration of The Dave Brubeck Quartet
  • 1958: Settled the nebulous quartet lineup into the "classic" Dave Brubeck Quartet with Paul Desmond (alto sax), Eugene "Senator" Wright (bass) and Joe Morello (drums).
  • 1959: Recorded and release "Time Out", the first jazz album to sell over a million copies.
  • 1967: Disbands the Quartet and forms a new Quartet with Jack Six (bass), Alan Dawson (drums) and Gerry Mulligan (bari sax).
  • 1996: Received a Grammy Lifetime Achievement award recognizing his work as an American jazz pianist and composer.
  • Died of heart failure in Norwalk, CT one day before his 92nd birthday.
